\# Authority and Trust Model



\## Status



Canonical



\## Purpose



The Authority and Trust Model defines the foundational principles of Parmana.



It explains:



\* What authority is

\* What trust is

\* Who defines authority

\* Who defines trust

\* How authority and trust interact

\* Why execution must be constrained by both



This model serves as the conceptual foundation for the Execution Trust Chain.



\---



\# Core Thesis



```text

AI has intelligence.



Humans have authority.



Intelligence generates actions.



Authority decides what is allowed.

```



Parmana exists because intelligence and authority are fundamentally different concepts.



\---



\# Intelligence



Intelligence is the ability to:



```text

Reason



Infer



Predict



Recommend



Plan



Generate actions

```



Examples:



```text

LLMs



Agents



AI Systems



Humans

```



Intelligence can generate possible actions.



Intelligence does not determine whether actions are authorized.



\---



\# Authority



Authority is the ability to determine:



```text

What is allowed



What is prohibited



Who may act



Under which conditions

```



Authority is a governance construct.



Authority is not intelligence.



\---



\# Authority Examples



Examples:



```text

Manager approves payment



Finance approves wire transfer



Compliance approves vendor



Organization defines policy

```



Authority originates from people and institutions.



\---



\# Trust



Trust is the organizational decision to accept a signal as valid evidence.



Trust answers:



```text

What evidence do we accept?

```



Examples:



```text

Manager Approval



KYC Verification



Risk Score



Fraud Score



Bank Confirmation



ERP Record

```



Trust is defined by the organization.



\---



\# Core Trust Principle



```text

Organizations decide what to trust.



Parmana ensures trusted signals satisfy policy before execution.

```



This is one of the central principles of Parmana.



\---



\# What Parmana Does Not Decide



Parmana does not decide:



```text

What is true



What is trustworthy



Which signals matter



Which policies should exist

```



Organizations make those decisions.



\---



\# What Parmana Does Decide



Parmana evaluates:



```text

Do trusted signals satisfy policy?

```



The result is an Authority Decision.

Authority emerges from policy evaluation.

\# AI and Trust



AI systems may generate signals.



Example:



```text

Fraud Risk Score



Document Classification



Intent Classification

```



Organizations may choose to trust those signals.



If trusted:



```text

AI Signal

&#x20;     ↓

Policy Evaluation

&#x20;     ↓

Authority Decision

```



If not trusted:



```text

AI Signal

&#x20;     ↓

Ignored

```



Parmana remains neutral.



\---



\# AI and Authority



AI may recommend actions.



Example:



```text

Release payment



Issue refund



Approve invoice

```



However:



```text

Recommendation ≠ Authority

```



Authority must be independently verified.
