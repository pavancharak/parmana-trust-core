# Task → Policy Runtime Architecture (Part 3)

## Policy as the Unit of Authority

A Task is a business action.

A Policy is an authority contract.

The Policy is the unit that determines:

```text
What signals are required?
What conditions must be satisfied?
What outcome is produced?
```

A policy does not execute actions.

A policy evaluates authority requirements.

---

## Signal-Centric Architecture

Traditional systems are identity-centric.

Example:

```text
User
 ↓
Role
 ↓
Permission
 ↓
Action
```

Parmana is signal-centric.

Example:

```text
Trusted Signals
 ↓
Policy Evaluation
 ↓
Authority Decision
```

Authority is determined from evidence.

Not assumptions.

Not hardcoded roles.

---

## Signals Are Facts

Signals represent verified facts.

Examples:

```text
identity_verified = true

beneficiary_verified = true

balance_available = true

fraud_score = 20

transfer_amount = 1000
```

Policies operate on facts.

Policies never operate on speculation.

---

## Trusted Signal Sources

Signals may originate from many systems.

Examples:

```text
Identity Providers

Banking Systems

Fraud Platforms

Risk Engines

Human Approvers

ERP Systems

CRM Systems

AI Systems
```

Organizations decide which sources are trusted.

Parmana does not decide trust.

Parmana evaluates trusted evidence.

---

## Human Authority Model

Parmana separates intelligence from authority.

Intelligence answers:

```text
What should happen?
```

Authority answers:

```text
What is allowed?
```

Example:

```text
AI
 ↓
Suggest payment

Policy
 ↓
Evaluate authority

Decision
 ↓
Approve or Reject
```

The AI may be intelligent.

The policy determines authority.

---

## Authority Is Evidence-Based

Authority is established through evidence.

Example:

```text
Identity Verified
+
Beneficiary Verified
+
Balance Available
+
Fraud Score Acceptable
=
Authority Granted
```

Without evidence there is no authority.

---

## Policy Evaluation Lifecycle

```text
Collect Signals
       ↓
Validate Signals
       ↓
Load Policy
       ↓
Evaluate Rules
       ↓
Generate Outcome
       ↓
Create Decision
```

Every decision can be reproduced later using the same:

```text
Policy Version
Signal Set
Evaluation Logic
```

This creates deterministic governance.

---

## Deterministic Decisions

A decision should always be reproducible.

Example:

```text
Policy Version:
1.0.0

Signals:
fraud_score = 20

Result:
approve
```

Future evaluations using the same policy and signals should produce the same result.

---

## Explainable Governance

Every outcome should have a reason.

Example:

```json
{
  "action": "approve",
  "reason": "payment_authorized"
}
```

Or:

```json
{
  "action": "reject",
  "reason": "fraud_score_exceeded_threshold"
}
```

This creates explainability.

---

## Override Model

Not every decision is binary.

Some decisions require escalation.

Example:

```json
{
  "action": "approve",
  "requires_override": true,
  "reason": "high_value_transfer_requires_approval"
}
```

This allows:

```text
Policy
 ↓
Human Review
 ↓
Execution
```

instead of immediate execution.

---

## Decision Object

A decision is the result of policy evaluation.

Example:

```json
{
  "action": "approve",
  "requires_override": false,
  "reason": "payment_authorized"
}
```

A decision is not execution.

A decision is authority evaluation output.

---

## Attestation Model

Decisions may be attested.

An attestation proves:

```text
A decision existed
A policy was evaluated
A result was produced
```

Attestations create cryptographic accountability.

---

## Verification Model

Verification determines whether a decision can be trusted.

Verification evaluates:

```text
Signatures
Trust Profiles
Threshold Requirements
Algorithms
```

Verification does not re-evaluate policy.

Verification validates trust.

---

## Receipt Model

A receipt is the audit artifact produced after verification.

Example:

```json
{
  "receiptId": "...",
  "decisionId": "...",
  "valid": true
}
```

Receipts provide:

```text
Auditability
Traceability
Evidence
Compliance
```

---

## Transparency Model

Receipts may be anchored in transparency logs.

Transparency logs provide:

```text
Tamper Evidence
Historical Proof
Independent Verification
```

This allows third parties to verify decision history.

---

Task

---

## Canonical Parmana Thesis

AI has intelligence.

Humans have authority.

Organizations decide what to trust.

Policies define what is required.

Trusted signals provide evidence.

Parmana evaluates those trusted signals against policy before execution.

Parmana makes authority verifiable and enforceable before execution.
