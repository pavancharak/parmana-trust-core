```text id="zsh6w9"

```



```text id="74j3f5"

```


## Why Federation Exists

Most trust systems assume:

```text id="0qpk0r"
Single Organization

Single Trust Store

Single Authority Model
```

Modern systems require:

```text id="kr4hmm"

Multiple Trust Domains

Multiple Authorities
```

Trust Federation enables interoperable trust.

## Federation Principle

Trust does not scale through centralization.

Trust scales through verifiable evidence.

Federation allows organizations to trust evidence rather than infrastructure.

## Canonical Parmana Federation Statement

Organizations decide what to trust.

Policies define authority requirements.

Trusted signals provide evidence.

Parmana evaluates those trusted signals against policy before execution.

Attestation creates cryptographic evidence of authority decisions.

Verification validates trust evidence.

Receipts preserve trust evidence.

Transparency logs preserve trust history.

Trust Federation enables organizations to independently verify and share trust across organizational boundaries.

Parmana makes authority verifiable, auditable, portable, federated, and enforceable before execution.
