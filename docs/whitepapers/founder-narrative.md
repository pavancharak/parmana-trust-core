\# Founder Narrative



\## Why Parmana Exists



For decades, software systems executed instructions.



The primary challenge was ensuring that only the right people had access to the right systems.



Identity solved that problem.



Security solved access control.



Observability solved visibility.



Then AI changed the architecture.



Systems stopped merely executing instructions and started generating actions.



This introduced a new problem.



The question was no longer:



"Who can access the system?"



The question became:



"Should this action be allowed?"



That question is fundamentally about authority.



\---



\# The Real Problem



AI systems can generate actions.



Organizations remain accountable for those actions.



An AI system may recommend:



\* Releasing a payment

\* Creating a vendor

\* Approving a refund

\* Executing a trade



The fact that the AI can generate the action does not mean the organization has authorized it.



Capability is not authority.



Intelligence is not permission.



\---



\# The Missing Layer



Enterprise architecture contains mature layers for:



\* Identity

\* Security

\* Observability



But there is no dedicated authority layer.



Today authority is fragmented across:



\* Business rules

\* Approval workflows

\* Access policies

\* Human processes

\* Audit controls



As autonomy increases, that fragmentation becomes a risk.



Organizations need a system that verifies authority before execution.



\---



\# The Insight



The breakthrough insight behind Parmana is simple:



Authority can be modeled.



Authority can be verified.



Authority can be proven.



Authority can become machine-verifiable.



Instead of treating authorization as a collection of workflows, Parmana treats it as a first-class system.
