# Executive Summary

The enterprise software stack is undergoing a fundamental transformation.

For decades, software systems were passive. Humans initiated actions, software executed instructions, and organizations relied on identity, access control, and audit logs to manage risk.

Artificial intelligence changes this model.

AI systems can now generate actions, recommend decisions, coordinate workflows, trigger processes, and operate with increasing levels of autonomy.

As the cost of generating actions approaches zero, a new challenge emerges:

**How do organizations ensure that actions are authorized before they are executed?**

Identity systems answer who someone is.

Security systems answer whether access is permitted.

Neither answers whether an action should be allowed.

This creates a new infrastructure requirement.

Organizations need a way to verify authority before execution and produce verifiable evidence proving that authority requirements were satisfied.

We call this category:

**Authority Infrastructure.**

The Authority Layer is becoming a foundational component of trustworthy autonomous systems.

# Why Identity Is Not Authority

Identity and authority are often treated as interchangeable concepts.

They are not.

Identity answers:

```text
Who are you?
```

Authority answers:

```text
What are you allowed to do?
```

Consider an employee with valid credentials.

Identity systems confirm:

```text
Authenticated
```

That does not prove:

```text
Authorized
```

The distinction becomes even more important for autonomous systems.

An AI agent may operate using valid credentials.

Identity remains valid.

Authority may not.

The future enterprise stack requires infrastructure that verifies authority independently from identity.

# Transparency by Design

Every verification event generates a receipt.

Every receipt generates a cryptographic hash.

Every hash is recorded in a transparency log.

```text
Verification
      ↓
Receipt
      ↓
Hash
      ↓
Transparency Log
```

Transparency becomes an architectural property rather than an operational process.

---

# Trust Anchors

Transparency logs provide evidence.

Trust anchors provide permanence.

Receipt hashes are organized into Merkle trees.

Merkle roots are signed and anchored.

```text
Receipt Hashes
       ↓
Merkle Root
       ↓
Trust Anchor
```

This creates an immutable record of authority verification activity.
