# Parmana Trust Core

## Part III — Authority Networks, Federated Trust & Autonomous Systems

---

# The Limitation of Single-System Trust

Most authorization systems assume a single organization.

```text
Organization
      ↓
Policy
      ↓
Decision
```

This works inside a company.

It breaks when authority spans:

* Banks
* Vendors
* Governments
* Autonomous agents
* Multi-party workflows

Modern systems increasingly operate across organizational boundaries.

---

# Federated Authority

Authority is rarely owned by a single system.

Consider a payment:

```text
Employee
    ↓
Manager
    ↓
Finance System
    ↓
Bank
```

Each participant contributes a piece of authority.

No single participant owns the entire decision.

---

# Federated Trust Model

Parmana treats authority as a network.

```text
Authority Source A
          ↓
Authority Source B
          ↓
Authority Source C
          ↓
Verification
```

Each authority source produces verifiable signals.

Parmana evaluates those signals before execution.

---

# Authority Signals

An authority signal may originate from:

```text
Human Approval
Software System
Identity Provider
Banking Platform
Compliance Engine
Risk Engine
AI System
```

The source is less important than trustworthiness.

Organizations define which signals are trusted.

---

# Canonical Trust Principle

Organizations decide what to trust.

Parmana ensures those trusted signals satisfy policy before execution.

This principle remains valid regardless of signal origin.

---

# Authority Graph

Instead of simple approval chains, Parmana models authority as a graph.

```text
Manager
    \
     \
      Decision
     /
Finance
     \
      \
       Risk Engine
```

Multiple independent authorities may contribute evidence.

---

# Authority Verification

Verification becomes:

```text
Authority Graph
        ↓
Policy Evaluation
        ↓
Verification Decision
```

Parmana evaluates whether required authority conditions are satisfied.

---

# Multi-Signature Authority

A policy may require multiple authorities.

Example:

```text
Manager Approval
AND
Finance Approval
```

Policy:

```json
{
  "threshold": 2,
  "requiredAuthorities": [
    "manager",
    "finance"
  ]
}
```

---

# Threshold Policies

Parmana supports threshold authority.

Example:

```text
3 of 5 approvers
```

```json
{
  "threshold": 3
}
```

This allows resilient governance without dependence on a single individual.

---

# Autonomous Systems

AI systems introduce a new challenge.

AI may generate actions.

AI should not generate authority.

Parmana separates intelligence from authority.

---

# Intelligence vs Authority

```text
AI
 ↓
Proposed Action

Human
 ↓
Authority
```

The AI proposes.

Authority approves.

Execution occurs only after verification.

---

# Autonomous Payment Example

```text
AI Agent
      ↓
Create Payment Request
      ↓
Parmana Verification
      ↓
Approved?
      ↓
Payment System
```

The AI never directly executes payment.

Authority remains external.

---

# Human Authority Layer

Every autonomous workflow ultimately resolves to human-defined authority.

```text
AI
 ↓
Policy
 ↓
Human Authority
 ↓
Verification
```

Authority remains accountable.

---

# Trust Domains

Large organizations contain multiple trust domains.

Examples:

```text
Finance
Legal
Operations
Security
```

Each domain may define its own policies.

Parmana evaluates policies within the appropriate trust domain.

---

# Cross-Domain Authority

Some actions require authority from multiple domains.

Example:

```text
Finance
AND
Legal
AND
Security
```

Parmana verifies all required authority signals before execution.

---

# Federated Trust Anchors

Multiple organizations may publish trust anchors.

```text
Organization A Root
Organization B Root
Organization C Root
```

These anchors become independently verifiable authority records.

---

# Trust Federation

Trust federation allows organizations to verify authority generated elsewhere.

```text
Organization A
        ↓
Trust Anchor
        ↓
Organization B
```

This enables:

* Vendor ecosystems
* Banking networks
* Supply chains
* Government integrations

---

# Authority Portability

Authority evidence becomes portable.

```text
Verification Receipt
        ↓
Transparency Proof
        ↓
Trust Anchor
        ↓
Portable Evidence
```

A verifier does not need access to the original system.

Only cryptographic evidence is required.

---

# Auditability

An auditor should be able to answer:

```text
Who had authority?
What policy applied?
What evidence existed?
When was it verified?
What executed?
Did execution match approval?
```

Parmana's evidence chain provides answers to each question.

---

# Enterprise Outcome

Traditional systems answer:

```text
Did someone approve?
```

Parmana answers:

```text
Was authority valid?
Was policy satisfied?
Can it be independently proven?
```

# Final Thesis

AI has intelligence.

Humans have authority.
